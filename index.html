🌍 The Discovery & Understanding of Earth

Unlike continents or planets discovered through exploration, Earth has always been humanity’s home. What changed over time was how we understood it — its shape, place in the universe, and life-sustaining power.

Thousands of years ago, many believed Earth was flat or carried by mythical creatures. Greek thinkers like Pythagoras and Aristotle introduced the idea of a spherical Earth.

In 150 CE, Ptolemy described a geocentric universe with Earth at the center. But in 1543, Copernicus changed everything with a heliocentric model. Later, Galileo used a telescope to confirm it, and Newton explained how it all worked.

In 1972, astronauts aboard Apollo 17 captured the first full image of Earth — the famous “Blue Marble.” For the first time, we saw our planet as one: round, fragile, and beautiful.

Today, with satellites, sensors, and science, we don’t just know Earth — we understand it. We realize how our choices affect it. The journey wasn’t about discovering Earth — it was about discovering our responsibility to protect it.

“Look again at that dot. That’s here. That’s home. That’s us.” – Carl Sagan
